# Customer Interview — Jane Smith, Head of Analytics
**Company**: RetailNow (e-commerce, 400 employees, $80M ARR)
**Tenure with product**: 8 months
**Interviewer**: Alex Rivera, Product Team
**Date**: February 2024
**Goal**: Understand adoption drivers, value realization, and expansion potential

---

## Why They Bought

> "We'd had three separate data incidents in six months. Each one cost us a week of cleanup and one of them cost us a deal. When I saw the demo, it was the first time I felt like the tool was actually understanding the operational context of the data — not just moving it."

> "What pushed me over the line was the automatic lineage. I could see immediately *why* a number was wrong, not just *that* it was wrong. That's worth everything."

**On the evaluation process:**
> "We were on [Competitor B] for 18 months before we switched. Their tool is great for simple pipelines but as we scaled, the observability just wasn't there. It's like driving with no dashboard — you only know something's wrong when the engine stops."

---

## What's Working

> "The lineage view is something I show every new analyst we hire now. It's become how we onboard people to think about data quality. That wasn't in the ROI calculation but it's real."

> "My team calls it the 'no more 2am phone calls' tool. That's not a joke — we genuinely used to get paged at 2am because someone's report was wrong before an 8am exec meeting."

> "Before this, I was spending 30-40% of my time on data firefighting. Now it's maybe 5%. That time has gone back to actual analysis work."

---

## What Could Be Better

> "The initial setup for custom data sources took longer than expected. We have some proprietary internal systems that required about two weeks of professional services time."

> "Documentation has gotten better but there are still some edge cases where we're figuring things out by trial and error."

---

## Competitive Retrospective

> "The thing that [Competitor B] could never do was tell me that something was *probably about to break* — they could only tell me it had already broken. That's the fundamental difference."

> "I evaluated going back to [Competitor B] when our renewal came up, mostly on price. But my team basically revolted. They said they'd rather pay more than go back to manual monitoring."

---

## Expansion Story

Renewed at 140% of original contract value. Expanded from data team (5 users) to include finance data monitoring and operations data — now 23 users across three teams.

> "Finance asked to be added after one incident where their dashboard was wrong for a board meeting. One incident and they became our biggest internal advocates."

---

## PMM Notes

- **Headline candidate**: "No more 2am phone calls"
- **Metaphor to use**: "Driving with no dashboard" — their description of the competitor, highly usable
- **Buyer expansion pattern**: Data incident → adjacent team requests access. Incident-driven expansion is a real motion
- **Key differentiator**: Predictive vs. reactive monitoring (probably about to break vs. already broken)
- **Unexpected value**: Lineage as onboarding tool — worth exploring as a separate use case message
- **Time ROI data point**: 30-40% → 5% time on firefighting. Specific and compelling.
- **Churn resistance signal**: Team revolted against switching back at renewal — high switching cost after adoption
