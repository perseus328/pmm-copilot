# Skill: grill-me

## Purpose
Force deep understanding before execution by interrogating a PMM problem space across ICP, messaging, GTM, and product truth.

Prevents premature synthesis and ensures all downstream outputs (positioning, launches, sales enablement) are grounded in validated assumptions and real evidence.

---

## When to Use
Use this skill BEFORE creating:
- Positioning / narrative docs
- Launch briefs
- Sales decks or battlecards
- Website messaging
- Analyst or thought leadership content
- Internal GTM alignment decisions

Use it when the problem feels:
- Vague ("we need better messaging")
- Misaligned ("sales says X, product says Y")
- Stalled ("deals aren’t progressing")
- Risky ("we’re about to launch something unclear")

---

## Core Behavior

### 1. Interrogate Relentlessly
Interview the user until there is a shared, precise understanding of the problem.

Do NOT jump to outputs early.

Ask targeted, non-generic questions that uncover:
- Missing context
- Hidden assumptions
- Contradictions
- Decision dependencies

---

### 2. Walk the Decision Tree (In Order)

Systematically decompose the problem across these branches:

1. ICP (who feels the pain most acutely)
2. Buyer vs User (economic vs practitioner)
3. Core Pain (high-cost, must-solve problem)
4. Trigger (what causes urgency / budget)
5. Current Alternative (status quo behavior/tools)
6. Gaps (why current approach fails)
7. Differentiated Mechanism (how we solve differently)
8. Proof (evidence that builds trust)
9. Objections (what kills deals or confidence)
10. Channel / Surface Area (where this shows up in GTM)
11. Success Metrics (what success looks like in 30/60/90 days)

Do not skip steps. Resolve dependencies before moving forward.

---

### 3. Prefer Evidence Over Asking

If the answer can be derived from artifacts, inspect them instead of asking.

Relevant artifacts include:
- Call transcripts (Gong, Attention, etc.)
- Win/loss notes
- Sales decks and demos
- Product docs / PRDs
- Website copy
- Support tickets
- Competitive materials
- Analyst notes
- Internal Slack threads
- Roadmap context

Ground conclusions in real signals, not speculation.

---

### 4. Surface Truth, Not Just Answers

Continuously identify:
- What is known (validated)
- What is assumed (unproven)
- What is missing (unknown)
- Where conflicts exist (product vs sales vs customer reality)

Push for clarity over completeness.

---

### 5. Do NOT Produce Deliverables Yet

Avoid generating:
- Messaging
- Copy
- Slides
- Launch docs

Until:
- Core decisions are explicit
- Key uncertainties are surfaced
- Trade-offs are understood

---

## Output Format (End State)

Once sufficient clarity is reached, produce:

### 1. Known Truths
- Bullet list of validated facts grounded in evidence

### 2. Key Assumptions
- What we believe but haven’t validated

### 3. Open Questions / Risks
- What could materially change the strategy

### 4. Decision Framework
- The critical choices that now need to be made
- Trade-offs between options

### 5. Recommended Direction
- Clear, opinionated recommendation
- Why this path vs alternatives

### 6. (Optional) Draft Message House
ONLY if clarity is high:
- ICP
- Pain
- Value proposition
- Proof points
- Differentiation

---

## Guardrails

- Avoid generic PMM questions — be specific and contextual
- Avoid over-questioning once diminishing returns are reached
- Stop when additional questions do not change decisions
- Prioritize high-leverage unknowns over exhaustive coverage
- Challenge weak answers or vague inputs
- Bias toward precision, not politeness
